# 5. LinkedIn Profile, Personal Brand, and Go-To-Market Plan

## Personal brand strategy

Your LinkedIn should make you look like a practical AI operations person, not a generic AI hype account.

Brand theme:

> Practical AI workflows for growing service businesses.

Your audience should repeatedly see you talk about:

- Business process bottlenecks
- Manual admin work
- Lead follow-up leakage
- CRM and spreadsheet chaos
- AI workflow examples
- Before/after process maps
- Simple automations
- AI agents with human approval
- Tool comparisons
- Founder/operator productivity

## Content pillars

### Pillar 1: Pain education

Teach buyers to recognize process leakage.

Post ideas:
- “Your CRM is not the problem. Your handoff process is.”
- “5 signs your business is ready for workflow automation.”
- “The hidden cost of slow lead follow-up.”
- “Why your team is busy but output is still slow.”

### Pillar 2: Workflow breakdowns

Show practical before/after workflows.

Post ideas:
- “Before/after: lead form to booked call.”
- “How I would automate client onboarding for an agency.”
- “A simple AI workflow for turning sales calls into proposal drafts.”
- “How to automate weekly KPI reporting without building a full dashboard first.”

### Pillar 3: Tool clarity

Help people understand tools without overwhelming them.

Post ideas:
- “Zapier vs Make vs n8n: which should a small business use?”
- “When not to use AI agents.”
- “Simple automation vs AI automation vs AI agents.”
- “Why your first automation should not be fully autonomous.”

### Pillar 4: Founder/operator point of view

Build trust by sounding like a business operator.

Post ideas:
- “AI should not replace your process. It should expose where the process is broken.”
- “The best automation projects start with boring admin tasks.”
- “A good AI consultant asks about error rates before asking about models.”

### Pillar 5: Offers and proof

Sell softly through examples.

Post ideas:
- “I’m opening 3 slots for AI Process Opportunity Audits this month.”
- “Here’s what you get in a workflow automation sprint.”
- “Demo: how a support triage assistant can reduce inbox chaos.”

## Outreach strategy

### Target people

- Agency founders
- Coaching/education business owners
- B2B service founders
- COOs/ops heads
- Sales heads in small companies

### Connection request

> Hi [Name], I’m connecting with founders/operators who are improving their internal workflows with AI and automation. I liked what you’re building at [Company]. Would be good to connect.

### Follow-up 1

> Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Quick question: are there any workflows in your business right now that still rely heavily on manual follow-up, spreadsheets, or copying data between tools?

### Follow-up 2 if they respond with a pain

> That’s exactly the kind of workflow I help map and automate. Usually I start with a short process audit to identify the highest-ROI workflow before building anything. Happy to share a simple checklist if useful.

### Soft offer

> If you want, I can do a paid AI Process Opportunity Audit where we map the workflow, score automation opportunities, and create a 30-day roadmap. No pressure to build anything until the ROI is clear.

## Stronger niche wedge from research

Best starting wedge if you want the clearest ROI story:

> AI workflow automation for recruiting and B2B sales/service teams.

Why:
- Clear pain
- Easy to explain
- Strong ROI
- Less regulatory complexity than healthcare/legal/finance
- Lots of repetitive text work
- Existing tools have APIs/integrations
- Buyers understand time = money

Initial positioning sentence:

> I help recruiting and sales teams save 10+ hours/week by automating candidate screening, lead research, follow-ups, CRM updates, and meeting prep.

## Beginner go-to-market plan

### First 30 days

- Pick one niche: agencies, coaching businesses, or B2B services
- Build 3 demo workflows
- Create website landing page
- Publish 20 LinkedIn posts
- DM 100 targeted prospects
- Offer 5 paid audits at launch pricing

### Days 31-60

- Convert 1-2 audits into workflow sprints
- Create case studies
- Improve offer based on objections
- Build one lead magnet
- Start a weekly newsletter or LinkedIn series

### Days 61-90

- Raise pricing
- Productize the most common workflow
- Add retainer offer
- Build referral partnerships with CRM consultants, web agencies, and accountants
