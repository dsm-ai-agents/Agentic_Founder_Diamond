The B2B Lead Generation Playbook

A practical, step-by-step system to book more meetings in the next 14 days. Includes ICP checklist, lead list setup, cold outreach templates, and a follow-up sequence.

01

What this playbook helps you do

Fix the four problems that make outbound feel inconsistent.

The usual problem

  • Targeting is too broad
  • Messaging is not specific enough
  • Follow-ups stop too early
  • There’s no simple tracking system

The fix

This playbook gives you a lightweight system to fix all four — fast. Use it as your operating system for the next 14 days.

02

The 3-step system

Simple enough to execute daily. Specific enough to improve with every reply.

1. Target

Target the right accounts with ICP clarity before writing messages.

2. Message

Send a message that earns a reply using problem + outcome + proof.

3. Follow up

Follow up like a system, not with vague “checking in” messages.

Goal for the next 14 days: build a list of 200–500 leads, send 20–50 messages per day, run a 4–6 touch sequence, and track replies + meetings booked.
03

ICP checklist

Fill this before you write a single message.

IndustryWrite the industries you serve best
Company sizeEmployees / revenue range
GeographyCountries, regions, or cities
Job titlesDecision makers and influencers
Current toolsTools/processes they likely use today
Top 3 painsProblems you solve
Top 3 outcomesBusiness results you deliver
DifferentiatorWhat makes you different in 1–2 lines
Hiring sales teamRecently raised fundingLaunching a new productExpanding into a new region
Rule: If you can’t describe “who + pain + outcome” in one sentence, your ICP is not ready.
04

Lead list setup

Build a clean list, not a random spreadsheet of contacts.

Where to find leads

  • LinkedIn Sales Navigator
  • Company directories and associations
  • Industry newsletters and event attendee lists
  • BuiltWith / similar tech lookup tools

Minimum fields to capture

  • First name, last name
  • Title and company
  • Website and LinkedIn URL
  • Work email
  • Phone, optional but valuable
  • Notes: trigger, tool used, recent post, etc.

Quality filter

Don’t add leads unless you can answer: “Why them, why now?”

05

Message framework

The only cold outreach structure you need.

Personalization

One relevant line.

Problem

What they likely face.

Outcome

What they want.

Proof

How you know / what you’ve done.

CTA

Simple and low-friction.

Keep it short

Clarity beats clever copy.

“Hey {{Name}} — saw {{relevant trigger}}. Many {{role}} teams struggle with {{pain}} which leads to {{cost}}. We help {{ICP}} achieve {{outcome}} in {{timeframe}} using {{method}}. Open to a quick 10-minute chat to see if this fits?”
06

Cold email templates

Copy, paste, and customize with specific triggers.

Template A — simple + direct

Subject: Quick question about {{Company}} Hi {{Name}}, Noticed {{trigger}} at {{Company}}. Are you currently working on {{relevant initiative}}? We help {{ICP}} get {{outcome}} by {{method}}. If it’s useful, I can share a 1-page plan for {{Company}}. Should I send it over? Thanks, {{Your Name}}

Template B — value first

Subject: {{Outcome}} for {{Company}}? Hi {{Name}}, I put together a quick checklist we use to help {{ICP}} improve {{outcome}}: - {{point 1}} - {{point 2}} - {{point 3}} If you tell me what you’re using for {{tool/process}} today, I can tailor it to {{Company}}. Worth sharing? — {{Your Name}}
07

LinkedIn DM templates

Start a conversation before you sell.

Connection request

“Hey {{Name}} — liked your post on {{topic}}. I work with {{ICP}} on {{outcome}}. Would love to connect.”

After they accept

“Thanks for connecting, {{Name}}. Quick question—are you currently focused on {{initiative}} at {{Company}}?”

If they reply “yes”

“Got it. We typically see {{pain}} show up when teams do {{context}}. If helpful, I can share the exact sequence we use to book meetings in {{industry}}. Want it?”

08

Follow-up sequence

Most meetings come from follow-ups. Make each touch add a reason to reply.

Day 1

Initial message

Send your first cold email or LinkedIn message.

Day 2

Add proof

“Quick add—recently helped {{similar company}} get {{result}} in {{timeframe}}. Worth exploring?”

Day 4

Offer asset

“I can share a short checklist/template for {{outcome}}—want it?”

Day 7

Clarify fit

“Before I close this out—are you the right person for {{topic}}, or should I speak to someone else?”

Day 10

Breakup message

“Looks like timing isn’t right. If you want, I can send the playbook and you can reach out when it’s a priority.”

09

KPI tracker

Track weekly. Keep the scoreboard simple.

New leads added
Messages sent
Replies
Positive replies
Meetings booked
Cost per meeting

Benchmarks

  • Cold outreach reply rate: 3–10%
  • Positive reply rate: 1–5%
  • Meetings booked depend on offer clarity + targeting
10

Your 14-day execution plan

Don’t optimize forever. Get the system moving.

Day 1–2

Finalize ICP + offer + messaging

Day 3–4

Build list of 200–500 leads

Day 5–14

Run daily outreach + follow-ups

Daily checklist

  • Add 20–50 leads
  • Send 20–50 messages
  • Follow up with yesterday’s non-responders
  • Track replies + meetings booked
