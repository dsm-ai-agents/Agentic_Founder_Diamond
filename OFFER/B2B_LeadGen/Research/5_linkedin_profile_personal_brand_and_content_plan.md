## Sample first post

Outbound is not broken.

Generic outbound is.

Most cold email campaigns fail before the first email is sent.

Not because the subject line is bad.
Not because the CTA is weak.
Not because the tool is wrong.

They fail because the list is lazy.

Wrong companies.
Wrong decision makers.
No buying trigger.
No context.
No reason for the prospect to care right now.

Then people blame cold email.

But cold email is just the delivery layer.

The real work happens before the email:
- Who exactly are we targeting?
- Why would they care now?
- What signal shows they might need this?
- What problem are they already experiencing?
- What proof or insight makes the message relevant?

This is where AI can actually help.

Not by blasting more emails.

But by researching accounts, finding signals, enriching decision makers, and helping sales teams send more relevant outreach.

The future of outbound is not “more volume.”

It is better targeting, better context, and better timing.

That is what I am building around.

Curious: what do you think kills most outbound campaigns — bad copy, bad lists, or bad offers?

## DM strategy

Do not pitch immediately.

Simple conversation opener:
> Hey [Name], noticed you work with [audience/niche]. I’m researching how B2B teams are handling outbound right now. Curious — are you currently relying more on referrals, inbound, or outbound for new sales conversations?

Follow-up if they respond:
> Makes sense. I’m seeing a lot of teams with the same issue: tools are available, but the actual outbound system is messy. If useful, I can share a quick checklist I use to spot pipeline leaks.

Soft offer:
> If you want, I can do a quick Loom walking through 2–3 outbound opportunities I see for your business. No pressure — it may give you a few ideas either way.
